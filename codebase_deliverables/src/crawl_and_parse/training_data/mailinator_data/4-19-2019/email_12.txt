Farming Your Way to Profits In December, the Hemp Farming Act of 2018 was signed into law as part of a U.S. agriculture bill. The new law allows for the farming of hemp in the U.S. for the first time in decades. Why hemp? The practical uses of hemp are almost innumerable. And hemp is a key component in creating CBD-based pharmaceutical and nutraceutical products. The signing of the bill could create a profit boom for medical marijuana companies with CBD products for sale on the open market… You see, hemp, a traditionally lucrative U.S. cash crop, was a casualty of the drug war due to its similarity to its psychoactive cousin, THC-bearing cannabis. Hemp, however, tends to be poor in THC while retaining appreciable levels of CBD. And CBD is a cannabinoid for which we’re discovering multiple health-related applications. To keep pace with this new development, the FDA will be working on how to better regulate legal hemp-derived CBD-containing supplements, foods and beverages. Once the dust settles, companies operating in the CBD space will be able to procure new sources of potentially cheaper hemp-derived CBD, rather than limit themselves to imports. This means less overhead and potentially higher profits. Which in turn will benefit you as a stakeholder in this space.
