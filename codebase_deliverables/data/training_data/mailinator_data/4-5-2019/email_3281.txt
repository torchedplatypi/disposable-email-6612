Midnight is a magical time -- and not just because you can start your binge of the second season of _Chilling Adventures of Sabrina_ at exactly that time on Friday. It 's the ultimate binge-watch power hour, and you're in luck because there are a ton of amazing shows and movies to check out this weekend. After you're done mainlining the occult, check out the Season 2 premiere of _Killing Eve_ , the _Crazy Ex-Girlfriend_ concert special, Brie Larson 's directorial debut, _Unicorn Store_ , and a modern adaptation of _Native Son_. Picking up where the holiday special left off, Sabrina ([Kiernan Shipka](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929I6)) and Harvey ([Ross Lynch](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929I7)) are still broken up which paves the way for Sabrina to dive headfirst into the occult at the Academy of Unseen Arts. Love triangles aren't the only challenges that pop up in her path; this season, Sabrina finally meets the devil and starts testing the boundaries of her morality. As batsh-- as it is hilarious, _Sabrina_ is ~the~ binge watch of the weekend **.** | **A CAT AND MOUSE GAME, BUT NOW WITH MORE STABBING** --- [ ![](http://cimg.tvgcdn.net/i/2019/02/09/ace26147-4a6a-45f3-84ca-56067ee6ef7a/190209-killing- eve.jpg) ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929I8) | [ **_Killing Eve_ Season 2 is back with a vengeance** ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929I9) --- **Premieres on Sunday at 8/7c on BBC America or AMC** _[Killing Eve](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ia)'s_ blockbuster first season was a heart-stopping, hilarious, and horrifying sprint to a brutal cliffhanger. Season 2 picks up right where Season 1 left off, and we see how our favorite serial killer Villanelle ([Jodie Comer](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ib)) survives a stab wound that she picked up from Eve ([Sandra Oh](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ic)), the MI6 agent hunting her down. Villanelle's struggle for survival kicks off another edge- of-your seat thriller, and dark comedy will keep you coming back for more **.** | **ENCORE** --- [ ![](http://cimg.tvgcdn.net/i/2018/08/21/96ae6932-0b9e-49e5-b41b-ad75cc2a145a/180821-shows- ending-this-season-crazy-ex-girlfriend.jpg) ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Id) | [ **_Crazy Ex-Girlfriend_ comes to a close with a concert special immediately following the series finale** ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ie) --- **Premieres Friday at 8/7c on CW** Closing the book on Rebecca 's ([Rachel Bloom](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929If)) story is no easy task. In four seasons, she's gone from the _[Crazy Ex- Girlfriend](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ig) _ to the kind of woman who knows herself deeply and loves herself for who she is rather than what she has. It's an emotional narrative that redefined the way we talk about mental health on TV, and so it's exceptionally fitting that it ends on a note of pure joy. The concert special makes it clear how much the cast and crew enjoyed making the show together and is truly a tribute to the genius of the series and the friendships that made it possible **.** | **HIGHER, FASTER, FURTHER** --- [ ![](http://cimg.tvgcdn.net/i/2019/03/19/5ff4ce9d-3596-4ee1-98ee-377ee048cc0a/5-unicorn- store.jpg) ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ih) | [ **Let 's meet Samuel L. Jackson at the _Unicorn Store_** ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ii) --- **Now on Netflix** _[Unicorn Store](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ij)_ is the fantastical directorial debut from the woman who is used to going above and beyond. [Brie Larson](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Ik), better known as Captain Marvel, steps behind the camera for the first time to deliver a surrealist adventure about a woman who leaves the drudergy of her office job behind to care for unicorn. If that sounds totally weird, it is; this movie has a very specific aesthetic that's not for everyone. However if any of this sounds up your alley, you'll find a lot to enjoy **.** | **END THE WEEKEND WITH BIG** --- [ ![](http://cimg.tvgcdn.net/i/2019/03/10/0cfe3993-3cd4-439a-8590-678463fd47f7/190310-native- son.jpg) ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Il) | [ **_Native Son_ but make it punk rock** ](http://newsletter.tvguide.com/re?l=D0I2r8jc2dIrr4l929Im) --- **Premieres Sunday at 10/9c on HBO** Richard Wright's iconic novel has been adapted before, but never like this. Rashid Johnson, a first-time director, takes the story of Bigger Thomas, a young black man trapped in poverty by systemic racism, and transports it to the modern era. Big (Ashton Sanders) is the kind of 2019 millennial who keeps a copy of Invisible Man next to a gun that he needs to survive in the South Side of Chicago.
