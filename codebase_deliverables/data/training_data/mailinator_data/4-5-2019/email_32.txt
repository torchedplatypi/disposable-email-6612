b'Heading to Auckland International Cultural Festival this Sunday?'
If you are experiencing problems viewing this email, click the link below to view an online version. https://e.at.govt.nz/mail/link/DA_pg9GZpi-Mt2DvoIv2Eg Use public transport to travel to the event and celebrate the cultural vibrancy of Auckland | [Click here](https://e.at.govt.nz/mail/link/C9BgBGVpwBW80NgeWVysRw) to view online --- [ ![Auckland Transport](https://engagemedia.azureedge.net/files/file/SOESnFjdOEKJKwjWT7qgyg) ](https://e.at.govt.nz/mail/link/6e41Cxt7Bs-eXUbsYB30Eg) | | ![](https://assets.ubiquity.co.nz/tspacer.gif) --- Auckland International Cultural Festival - Sunday 7 April ![](https://assets.ubiquity.co.nz/tspacer.gif) Don’t miss the 20th year of Auckland’s biggest celebration of cultural diversity! In one unforgettable day, Auckland International Cultural Festival brings together the cultural vibrancy of more than 50 countries offering a vast array of food, entertainment, stalls and activities. Gather your friends and family, and head out to Mt Roskill to experience tastes, sights and sounds from around the world. **Sunday 7 April, 10:00am-5:00pm at Mt Roskill War Memorial Park** Auckland International Cultural Festival is brought to you by Auckland Council. For more information please **[click here](https://e.at.govt.nz/mail/link/gxoxP4rbLrMum5ctDMLY8Q)**. **Plan your travel** **Train** * Catch a train into Britomart, then transfer to the 25B/25L bus services departing from Queen Street, or the 24B bus service departing from Victoria Street. * Sylvia Park, Penrose or Mt Albert train stations – connect to the 66 bus service (travels between Mt Wellington and Pt Chevalier via Mt Albert Road). * Mt Eden train station – connect to the 25B/25L bus services (travels between Blockhouse Bay/Lynfield and the City Centre via Dominion Road. * New Lynn, Otahuhu or Onehunga train stations – connect to the 670 bus service (travels between New Lynn and Otahuhu via Onehunga). * Normal fares/passes apply for train travel. **Bus** * If travelling from the city, the 25B/25L services regularly depart from Queen Street outside the St James (stop 7058) and travel via Dominion Road. The 24B also departs from Victoria Street and travels via Sandringham Road. * You can also easily get to the event using one of the following crosstown buses. The frequent 66 service travels between Mt Wellington and Pt Chevalier via Mt Albert Road every 15 minutes. The nearest bus stop to the event is on Mt Albert Road (by May Road). The 670 service travels between New Lynn and Otahuhu via Onehunga every 30 minutes. The nearest bus stop to the event is on Denbigh Avenue (by May Road). * Normal fares/passes apply for bus travel. **Car** * There is very limited parking at the venue. On-street parking restrictions will be in place around the event. We recommend using public transport to travel to the event. To find out all you need to know about getting to Auckland International Cultural Festival before you travel, please visit our **[website](https://e.at.govt.nz/mail/link/qkTaDEjZI3VzpJEaF8BWJg)**.
